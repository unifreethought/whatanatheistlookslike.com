Coming Out As An Atheist

Coming out as an atheist can be quite intimidating. For some it's as simple and non-controversial as posting a Facebook status. For others it can mean problems with family, friends, and separation from the community that religion provides.

Yet coming out can be very important. We only have this one life to live, and people should not have to hide who they are from the people they care about. They should not be afraid to keep their beliefs secret for fear of backlash. Being an atheist is not wrong. It does not make you a bad person. If people are offended, then that is their problem, and they need to learn to deal with people who do not share their beliefs and values. Of course there are times when people should only come out to certain people. If coming out means being kicked out of your house, or facing emotional or physical abuse, then it's probably not a good idea. Things like that do happen, although rarely.

This About.com webpage offers some good advice for individuals thinking of outing themselves as an atheist. It's worth reading if you're partially or wholly closeted about your beliefs.
